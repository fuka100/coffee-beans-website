Blue Mountain

Jamaican Blue Mountain coffee beans are produced in Jamaica. It has a smooth acidity and a clean taste with a slight bitterness. Its aroma is said to give off a floral scent along with scents of sweet nuts and herbs. It is said that the flavour of the coffee is mild, with a very pleasant sweetness that is almost creamy. What makes this coffee tasty is the unique location and microclimate where the beans grow. Blue Mountain coffee beans are cultivated in the mountains. 85% of the mountains are forest, and the forest provides shade for the coffee to grow. The region has the amazing conditions for growing the best coffee in the world.

Brazil

Brazil coffee beans are grown in Brazil. Brazil is the world's leading producer of coffee beans, producing more than 40% of the world's coffee. The microclimate in certain regions of Brazil is one of the conditions that allow for the production of excellent coffee. In particular, several areas within the Cerrado, Matas de Minas, Mogiana, and Sul de Minas regions are famous for growing great coffee. Good Brazilian coffee is low in acidity and has a nutty, sweet flavour and a bittersweet, chocolaty roast taste. Its mild, soft taste makes it a great base for making flavoured coffee.
